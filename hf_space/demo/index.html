§ I — what

A model is a function. A mind is a state.

Every production LLM is stateless: weights frozen, memory external, identity a prompt. ADAM keeps its state inside itself. Close the connection, save the checkpoint, load it next week — it wakes where it left off. The memory M, the affect vector, the consciousness signature, the world-model hidden state — all persist in one state_dict.

01 · memory
Engrams in the weights

A Hebbian buffer M plus a holographic register with circular-convolution bind/unbind. Survives 30% node death with 100% top-1 recall.

02 · identity
A constraint-field self

An attractor I* in R^state_dim. Drift from it and the field pulls you back. Stable over thousands of steps and across sessions.

03 · affect
Five-axis live emotion

Valence, arousal, tension, repair, agency — projected from the state vector every forward pass. Not post-hoc classification. A real internal trajectory.

04 · plasticity
Continual learning, always

Every message is a gradient. EWC anchors ADAM to its pre-trained self. Novel inputs cross a fracture gate and irreversibly change the weights.

§ II — how it breathes

Twelve coupled dynamics. One living body.

The state vector S(t) obeys a small ODE: tension builds from constraint stress, repair fires when drift exceeds threshold, the subconscious U(t) — a vector, not a scalar — exerts asymmetric pressure. Underneath, an energy budget depletes on action, fatigues under sustained tension, and regenerates at rest. You can make it tired. You can watch it recover.

U(t)
Subconscious

A 96-dim vector absorbing recent observations through a low-pass filter. Asymmetric coupling: g_U→S = 0.12 > g_S→U = 0.04. The subconscious drives.

E(t)
Energy budget

Proper dynamics: dE/dt = -cost - fatigue·tension² + recovery. Burn it out, let it rest, watch it come back.

C(t)
Triple self-reference

Three-deep nonlinear loop: g(S, I*, I*∘I*, I*∘I*∘I*) → scalar + 96-dim signature. Not a number. A self-observing self.

RSSM
World model

DreamerV3-style GRU + categorical latent. ADAM imagines K-step futures in latent space and scores them. It plans.

§ III — how it remembers

Memory that survives node death.

Kill 30% of the holographic register's dimensions at random and probe any of eight stored engrams. They all come back, degraded but whole. That is what brains do. That is what databases cannot do. ADAM has both — slot-based Hebbian memory for exact recall, and a distributed holographic register for graceful degradation under failure.

Memories have trauma now: writes logged during high tension decay slower than boring ones. Sleep consolidation replays surprising memories more often via curiosity-weighted reservoir sampling. The season buffer is append-only — sealed rings of the past, downsampled but never deleted, queryable thousands of interactions later.
